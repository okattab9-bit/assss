Meteor Simulation Data

All the key formulas, laws, and parameters used to calculate meteor impacts and effects on Earth, presented clearly and visually.

Newton's Laws of Motion

Determine the meteor's trajectory and acceleration.

  • Force: F = m × a
  • Velocity: v = v₀ + a × t
  • Displacement: s = v₀ × t + 0.5 × a × t²

Kinetic Energy

Energy released on impact.

  • KE = 0.5 × m × v²
  • m = mass of meteor (kg)
  • v = velocity (m/s)

Impact Crater Estimation

Approximate crater size after impact.

  • D ≈ 1.8 × size × (KE / 1e12)^0.25 (meters)
  • size = meteor diameter (m)
  • KE = kinetic energy (J)

Meteor Parameters

Factors affecting the simulation.

  • Density by composition: rocky (~3000 kg/m³), metallic (~7800 kg/m³), icy (~1000 kg/m³)
  • Entry altitude (km)
  • Impact direction: N, S, E, W, NE, NW, SE, SW
  • Time to impact: altitude / speed

Simulation Tips

How to adjust parameters for realistic results:

  • Larger meteors = larger craters & more energy release
  • Higher speed = exponential increase in energy
  • Metallic composition = heavier & faster impact
  • Entry angle affects crater shape & debris spread
